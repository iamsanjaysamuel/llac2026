About Sanjay Samuel.

Sanjay Samuel is a leadership practitioner, author, and cricket enthusiast who believes the cricket field holds some of the most profound lessons in team leadership the world has ever seen.

Having spent years working with first-time managers and watching them struggle with the same challenges — Sanjay wrote Lead Like a Captain to be the guide he wished someone had given him on Day 1.

"The captain doesn't just play the game — they read the game. That's the difference between a manager and a leader."

The book condenses 27 of cricket's most powerful captaincy moments into actionable leadership lessons — one per chapter, one action per week.

Core beliefs

What Sanjay believes about leadership.

01
Leadership is learned, not born
Great captains weren't born knowing how to read the pitch. They observed, practised, failed, and adapted. So can you.
02
Action beats theory every time
A captain who reads 10 leadership books but never applies anything leads no better than someone who's read none. Do the thing.
03
Teams make leaders, not the reverse
A captain is as good as their eleven. Your job isn't to be the best player — it's to make everyone around you perform better.
04
Composure is a superpower
The best captains are the calmest in the final over. Your team watches how you react when things go wrong. Make it count.
05
Context is everything
No two pitches are the same. No two teams are the same. Good leadership adapts. Great leadership anticipates.
06
Legacy beats trophies
The greatest captains are remembered not for their wins but for how they made their players feel and who they helped become.
